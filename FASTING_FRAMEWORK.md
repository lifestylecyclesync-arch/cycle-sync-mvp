## Key Insights

### **Power Phase (Days 1-10)**
- **Hormones**: Low ‚Üí Rising estrogen, low progesterone
- **Metabolic State**: Building capacity, recovering from menstruation
- **Fasting**: Extended fasting tolerable (16-24 hours)
- **Why**: Body can handle fasting while rebuilding; increasing estrogen supports metabolic rate
- **Includes**: Menstrual recovery + early Follicular growth

### **Manifestation Phase (Days 11-15)**
- **Hormones**: Peak estrogen, LH surge at ovulation
- **Metabolic State**: Highest energy & metabolism of entire cycle
- **Fasting**: Peak fasting window (24-48 hours)
- **Why**: Peak estrogen = peak metabolism = best time for extended or water fasting
- **Includes**: Late Follicular through Ovulation window

### **Power Phase (Days 16-19)**
- **Hormones**: Declining estrogen, rising progesterone
- **Metabolic State**: Still elevated but declining
- **Fasting**: Extended fasting still manageable (16-24 hours)
- **Why**: Window of opportunity before progesterone fully dominates; still has energy
- **Includes**: Early Luteal phase, first days of progesterone rise

### **Nurture Phase (Days 20-28)**
- **Hormones**: Low estrogen, high progesterone
- **Metabolic State**: Lower energy, increased hunger, higher caloric needs
- **Fasting**: No fasting - eating windows
- **Why**: Progesterone increases caloric needs and hunger; fasting stresses the body
- **Includes**: Mid to late Luteal phase
